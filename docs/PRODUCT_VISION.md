# TableUp Product Vision

## Why TableUp Exists

Most households do not fail at cooking because they lack recipes. They fail because they do not know what is already at home, what is about to expire, what can be combined into dinner, and what is worth cooking tonight.

TableUp exists to connect inventory, recipes, expiration dates, ingredient knowledge, and household behavior into one practical kitchen assistant.

## User Pain Points

Users often struggle with:

- Forgetting what is in the fridge or freezer
- Buying duplicate ingredients
- Letting food expire
- Not knowing what to cook with current ingredients
- Having too many recipe choices
- Not knowing whether substitutions are acceptable
- Maintaining inventory manually
- Coordinating food across household members

## Problem Being Solved

TableUp is not primarily a recipe app.

The core product problem is:

```text
Given what this household has right now, what is the best thing to cook tonight?
```

This requires:

- Accurate enough inventory
- Structured recipes
- Ingredient aliases and matching
- Expiration and storage knowledge
- Recommendation ranking
- Household memory over time

## Target Market

Initial market:

- Chinese users living in the United States
- Users who shop at Asian grocery stores
- Families that cook at home and want less waste
- Users who need both Chinese and English ingredient handling

Expansion market:

- General US household cooking
- Family inventory sharing
- Waste reduction and meal decision support

## Product Differentiation

TableUp should differentiate through:

- Household inventory awareness
- Expiration-aware recipe recommendation
- Structured recipe data instead of plain content
- Ingredient knowledge graph
- Clean alias/modifier matching for Chinese and English ingredients
- Dynamic substitution based on category, tags, and verified rules
- Family kitchen memory

Generic AI can generate recipes, but TableUp should know the user's pantry, fridge, family habits, and waste risk.

## Long-Term Vision

TableUp should become a household kitchen memory system.

Over time it should remember:

- What the household buys often
- What expires often
- What recipes the household actually cooks
- Which ingredients are disliked or avoided
- Which recipes feel too hard
- Which meals are good for leftovers
- Which shopping decisions reduce waste

## Product Philosophy

TableUp should help users:

- Waste less food
- Buy less duplicate food
- Think less about dinner
- Cook more confidently
- Coordinate household food decisions

The app should optimize for successful home dinners, not endless browsing.

## Subscription Direction

Users should not pay for generic recipes.

Paid value should come from:

- Fridge Rescue Plan
- Smart expiration alerts
- Unlimited scan/receipt capture
- Family Kitchen Sync
- Smart grocery lists
- Weekly waste and savings reports
- Personalized household memory

Simple framing:

```text
Free: find what to cook tonight.
Pro: manage the whole fridge over time.
```
